### МОНГОЛЬСКАЯ ИМПЕРИЯ (MONGOL EMPIRE)

#### 🧘 ШАМАНИЗМ, ВЕРОВАНИЯ И РИТУАЛЫ (SHAMANISM & BELIEFS)

1. **Tengri (Тэнгри) / «Вечное Синее Небо»**  
    — The Mongols believed in **Tengri** (also Gok Monggke Tenggeri), the 'Blue Sky' or 'Eternal Heaven', a supreme protector god who granted them the right to rule the world. Prayers were offered simply by standing in the open air, removing one's hat and belt as a sign of submission, without the need for temples or ceremonies[](https://www.worldhistory.org/article/1469/religion-in-the-mongol-empire/?mc_cid=3fe6a97a14&mc_eid=f3436).
    
2. **Etugen (Этугэн) — Богиня Земли**  
    — In Mongol belief, the Earth or Mother Earth goddess, known as **Etugen** (aka Itugen), represented fertility and was venerated alongside the sky god[](https://www.worldhistory.org/article/1469/religion-in-the-mongol-empire/?mc_cid=3fe6a97a14&mc_eid=f3436). While Tengri was the dominant male sky deity, Etugen was his essential female counterpart, embodying the generative power of the land.
    
3. **Ovoo (Обоо) — каменные кучи**  
    — Specific natural locations, especially **ovoo** (stone cairns on mountaintops or hill peaks), were regarded as favourable spots for contacting spirits[](https://www.worldhistory.org/article/1469/religion-in-the-mongol-empire/?mc_cid=3fe6a97a14&mc_eid=f3436). Travelers would traditionally circle an ovoo three times and add a stone to the pile to ensure a safe journey.
    
4. **Табу на кровь и реки**  
    — Mongol taboos included **not shedding royal blood** (the soul was believed to reside in the blood and bones), **not urinating or washing in rivers** (to avoid offending water spirits), **not stepping on the threshold of a yurt**, and never putting a knife anywhere near a fire[](https://www.worldhistory.org/article/1469/religion-in-the-mongol-empire/?mc_cid=3fe6a97a14&mc_eid=f3436).
    
5. **Black Shamanism vs. Yellow Shamanism**  
    — Black shamanism worked with **evil spirits** and communicating with the dead, while white shamanism worked with spirits of the upper world. Black shamans were believed to be able to **turn into animals, fly, and go into trances**[](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_shamanism).
    
6. **99 Tengris**  
    — The Mongol pantheon was organized as a complex hierarchy of **99 tengri** (sky spirits): 55 benevolent or "white" and 44 terrifying or "black". The tengri were called upon only by leaders and great shamans[](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_shamanism).
    
7. **Скапулимантия (гадание на бараньих лопатках)**  
    — Divination before campaigns was serious business: Chinggis Khan's adviser **Yelü Chucai** conducted scrying sessions, and his calculations were compared to the Khan's own from _scapulimancy_ — reading the cracks formed in charred sheep shoulder blades[](https://www.historytoday.com/chinggis-khan-versus-unicorn).
    
8. **Волшебные приспособления для реки**  
    — Moving water such as rivers was thought capable of **blocking and even nullifying evil** spirits, acting as a spiritual barrier[](https://www.worldhistory.org/article/1469/religion-in-the-mongol-empire/?mc_cid=3fe6a97a14&mc_eid=f3436). This belief led to ritual practices of tossing special talismanic objects into flowing water before major battles.
    

#### ✨ СВЕРХЪЕСТЕСТВЕННЫЕ СУЩЕСТВА (SUPERNATURAL CREATURES)

9. **Mangas (Мангас) — многоголовый демон**  
    — The _Mangas_ is a demonic creature in Mongol mythology that can have either one head or multiple heads. In the _Jangar_ epic, Mangas with many heads often denote foreign emperors and enemies of the Mongols[](https://library.dctabudhabi.ae/eds/detail?db=edsdoj&an=edsdoj.17dd85716584986843343dc293c94db).
    
10. **Чингисхан и единорог**  
    — In 1224, before entering northern India, Chinggis Khan encountered a **single-horned green animal with a horse's tail** that could speak like a human. It warned him: "Your lord should return early." His adviser identified it as a _jueduan_ (rhinoceros in disguise), saying it "abhors the taking of life" — the Mongol army withdrew[](https://www.historytoday.com/chinggis-khan-versus-unicorn).
    
11. **Священные животные**  
    — Among the most important symbolic animals in Mongol mythology were the **wolf, falcon, deer, and horse**. The wolf, in particular, appears in the epic of King Gesar, which is shared across Central Asia and Tibet[](https://webarchiveweb.wayback.bac-lac.canada.ca/web/20210518153845/https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mongol_mythology).
    
12. **Luz (Луз) — водяные змеи-духи**  
    — _Luz_ (also _Luu_) are water snakes and dragon spirits in Mongol folklore that function as **divine creatures which help and protect**. They act as assistants, mediators, and messengers from the other world to communicate with the real world.
    

#### 👑 АРМИЯ, ЖИЗНЬ ВО ДВОРЦЕ И БЫТ (ARMY, PALACE LIFE & DAILY LIFE)

13. **Женщины-воительницы и правительницы**  
    — Pre-Buddhist Mongol women enjoyed significant independence: while men raided cities, **women managed tents, children, and livestock — they rode horses, hunted, and were socially autonomous**. A woman abducted or raped was not stigmatised as impure.
    
14. **Royal carts driven by women**  
    — In the Mongol Empire, **women drove the carts**; men were not permitted to ride in them unless they were sick. Women also made the felt tents that the carts carried and the Mongols lived in.
    
15. **Töregene Khatun**  
    — **Töregene** (c. 1241–1246) was the sixth wife of Ögedei Khan, the second Great Khan. After her husband's death, she ruled as **regent** of the Mongol Empire for five years, purging her opponents and appointing her own supporters to key positions — all without ever being formally crowned.
    
16. **Активная роль женщин в управлении империей**  
    — In the Mongol empire, Mongol royal ladies held significant political and economic status. They were mainly responsible for **financing, regulating foods, and packaging loads**.
    
17. **Управление гаремом через «Кхатун»**  
    — The term **"Khatun"** designated the empress or queen. Unlike the secluded harems of China and the Ottoman Empire, high-ranking Mongol women could own property, manage their own finances, and participate in state affairs.
    

#### 🎮 ПЕРЕОСМЫСЛЕНИЕ В СОВРЕМЕННЫХ ИГРАХ И МЕДИА (MODERN INTERPRETATIONS)

18. **Ghost of Tsushima (2020)**  
    — The Mongol invaders in the game are depicted as **monstrous and dehumanized** through language barriers and brute force, framed to contrast with a "pure" Japanese identity. In reality, the invading force was mostly composed of Han Chinese and Korean vassals[](https://www.taylorfrancis.com/chapters/edit/10.5117/9789463725682_CH01/honor-died-beach-constructing-japaneseness-monstrosity-ghost-tsushima-dom-ford-joleen-blom).
    
19. **Манга/аниме «A Witch's Life in Mongol» (Tenmaku no Jādūgar)**  
    — Set in the 13th-century Yeke Mongol Ulus, the story follows **Fatima**, a young woman from Persia who becomes an advisor to **Töregene**, Ögedei Khan's sixth wife. Together, they enter the arena of global politics within the imperial court. Science SARU is producing the anime[](https://gamerant.com/jaadugar-a-witch-in-mongolia-anime-science-saru-anime-announced/).
    
20. **Genghis Khan в поп-культуре**  
    — In Western media, Genghis Khan is often depicted as a **ruthless barbarian**, while in Mongolian and Asian contexts, the emphasis is on his role in **tribal unification, legal reforms, and facilitation of Eurasian trade networks**[](https://grokipedia.com/page/Genghis_Khan_in_popular_culture).
    
21. **«Ведьмак» (The Witcher)**  
    — The nomadic peoples of the northern kingdoms in The Witcher, such as the **Aen Elle elves**, show traces of Mongol military tactics (fast cavalry, encirclement) and shamanic elements of sky-worship. Even the concept of the **Wild Hunt** can be linked to steppe beliefs about ghost riders.

### 🇹🇷 ОСМАНСКАЯ ИМПЕРИЯ (OTTOMAN EMPIRE)

#### 🧙 МАГИЧЕСКИЕ СУЩЕСТВА, ПОВЕРЬЯ И РИТУАЛЫ (SUPERNATURAL CREATURES & FOLK MAGIC)

1. **Mhachkay (Meçkey) — вампир с двумя душами**  
    — A person born with **two hearts and two souls** was believed to be a _Mhachkay_ — an undead creature that flies at night as an owl, sucks blood, and eats the insides of travelers. To prevent rising, the corpse must be **decapitated and buried separately** from its head[](https://en.wikipedia.org/?curid=40757828).
    
2. **Талисманные рубашки**  
    — Entire costumes known as _talismanic shirts_ were covered with **Qur'anic verses, magic formulas, and numerological charms** made of letters and numbers, which were thought to have magical powers. Some belonged to Sultan Bayezid I himself[](https://islamicart.museumwnf.org/database_item.php?id=object;isl;tr;mus01;18;en&cp]).
    
3. **Магические квадраты и талисманы**  
    — Complex **magic squares and talismans** were manufactured in great quantities across the Ottoman Empire. Such practices were common among both the elite and vernacular Sufi orders, especially those based on the properties of **Arabic letters**[](https://www.jhiblog.org/2024/10/14/mysticism-power-and-rationalism-in-the-ottoman-mind-an-interview-with-marinos-sariyannis/#sbe-follow-wrap).
    
4. **Джинны в османской народной магии**  
    — Folk magic in both urban and rural Ottoman settings followed demonic/ritual magic, mainly based on **jinn invocations** and lettrist techniques using talismans or alphanumeric calculations — widely used by common folk[](https://www.jhiblog.org/2024/10/14/mysticism-power-and-rationalism-in-the-ottoman-mind-an-interview-with-marinos-sariyannis/#sbe-follow-wrap).
    
5. **Вурдалаки и янычары**  
    — Vampire-type creatures were mentioned in 16th-century _fatwas_ of Shaykh al-Islām Ebûssuûd Efendi. Vampires were also **associated with janissaries** as propaganda to legitimize the abolition of the Janissary Corps in 1826 (the so-called "Witches of Tirnova")[](https://www.ceeol.com/search/article-detail?id=685884).
    
6. **Русали (Rusali)**  
    — In Ottoman Balkan folklore, _Rusali_ (singular _Rusala_) were female water spirits with roots in pre-Christian Slavic beliefs. By the Ottoman era, their image had fused with both Slavic myth and Turkic notions of perilous feminine water beings who could grant rain or cause drownings.
    
7. **Hobur (Хобур)**  
    — _Hobur_ is a Turkic mythological creature associated with domestic spaces, similar to a house spirit. In Ottoman Anatolia, _hobur_ beliefs merged with Slavic _domovoy_ traditions, creating unique hybrid domestic spirit lore among Balkan Muslims[](https://ibn.idsi.md/sites/default/files/j_nr_file/REC_Vol_17_2015.pdf#67#22).
    
8. **Карынкажан / Karakanzal**  
    — _Karakanzal_ (also _karakondzul_, _karakoncol_) is a one-legged, hairy, blind demon that only appears during the Twelve Days of Christmas (between Christmas and Epiphany) and attacks people who wander outside at night.
    

#### 💍 ГАРЕМ, ЖИЗНЬ ВО ДВОРЦЕ, СЕКС И РОДЫ (HAREM, PALACE LIFE, SEX & CHILDBIRTH)

9. **Три группы наложниц**  
    — Concubines living in the Harem were separated into three groups: **Acemi (unskilled), Kalfa (qualified) and Usta (skilled)**. Each woman received a salary from the Royal treasury and dressed more elaborately than ordinary women[](https://www.hurriyetdailynews.com/exhibition-to-reveal-real-life-in-ottoman-seraglio-20900).
    
10. **Женщины в османском обществе**  
    — Even elite women shared the risks of **pregnancy, childbirth and epidemic diseases** with townswomen of the subject class and to a certain extent, with village women as well.
    
11. **Специальный стул для родов**  
    — Before childbirth, a concubine was taken to a special room where a midwife and a **special birthing chair** awaited her. Male doctors could not look at or touch the woman, so they **stood behind a screen**, while the midwife described the process to them[](https://www.kinoafisha.info/articles/vrachu-nelzya-bylo-otkryvat-glaza-strannosti-rodov-v-osmanskom-gareme_id77612_a14813648/?utm_campaign=rec).
    
12. **Три класса повитух**  
    — There were **three classes of midwives** in the Ottoman State: **Saray Ebeleri** (palace midwives), **Kibar Ebeleri** (elite-class midwives), and **Halk Ebeleri** (folk midwives). They helped with infertility treatment and abortion, using both religious methods and herbal medicines.
    
13. **Главный черный евнух (Kizlar Agha)**  
    — The _Kizlar Agha_ (also known as the "**Chief Black Eunuch**" because of the Nilotic origin of most aghas) was the head of the eunuchs responsible for guarding the Imperial Harem. The chief eunuch had a considerable status, second only to the Grand Vizier[](https://webarchiveweb.wayback.bac-lac.canada.ca/web/20211222182706/https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ottoman_Imperial_Harem).
    
14. **Власть и интриги гарема**  
    — The Imperial Harem wielded **considerable political authority** in Ottoman affairs, especially during the long period known as the **Sultanate of Women (approximately 1533 to 1656)**. Multiple historians claim the sultan was frequently lobbied by harem members[](https://webarchiveweb.wayback.bac-lac.canada.ca/web/20211222182706/https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ottoman_Imperial_Harem).
    

#### 🌟 ОЧЕНЬ НИШЕВЫЕ ФАКТЫ (VERY NICHE FACTS)

15. **Калфа как квалифицированные наложницы**  
    — The term **"Kalfa"** in the Ottoman Harem refers to a class of qualified women who had **specialized skills** — such as embroidery, music, or calligraphy. These women were considered more valuable than ordinary concubines and often served as mentors to younger girls.
    
16. **"The Great Eunuch of Constantinople"**  
    — According to some accounts, the Sultan had an **abnormal sexual interest** that favored the **fattest concubine** in the Ottoman Empire, instead of other beautiful women. His eunuchs facilitated these unusual preferences.
    
17. **Свадьбы в гареме в записках Зары-бека**  
    — The travel notes of Zara-bek describe that weddings in the harem were celebrated with **special cakes of honey and poppy seeds**. Men were allowed to dance only with dancers separated by a **huge transparent curtain**.
    
18. **Управление гаремом через чёрных евнухов**  
    — The harem's garden was strictly guarded. The Sultan's wives, daughters, slaves, servants, and concubines climbed into their carriages and set off for excursions only under the supervision of the **Chief Eunuch**, amid cries of "Allahu Akbar!".

### 🌲 СЛАВЯНСКИЕ ПЛЕМЕНА (SLAVIC TRIBES)

#### 🧘 ЯЗЫЧЕСТВО, ВЕРОВАНИЯ И РИТУАЛЫ (PAGAN BELIEFS & RITUALS)

1. **Родовые духи каждого строения**  
    — Every structure was believed to be inhabited by its particular spirit: the **domovoy** in the house, the **ovinnik** in the drying-house, the **gumenik** in the storehouse. Belief in spirits dwelling in the posts and beams of houses survives to this day in some regions[](https://www.britannica.com/topic/Slavic-religion).
    
2. **Leshy — хозяин леса**  
    — The _leshy_, a forest spirit, was believed to **regulate and assign prey to hunters**. In later ages, it became the protector of flocks and herds. In early 20th-century Russia, the spirit was offered bran and eggs to ensure the safe return of a cow or herdsman[](https://www.britannica.com/topic/Slavic-religion).
    
3. **Pagan sacrifices in the open**  
    — Pagan Russians did not build special temples for their gods, but erected their **idols in the open on slopes and hills**. Sacrifices of animals, grain, and food were offered to the gods; in times of war, captives were slaughtered in their honour.
    
4. **Три компонента славянской языческой системы**  
    — The Slavic pagan system had a **three-component organization**: the world of gods, the world of ancestors, and the world of nature spirits. Prayers, divinations, and sacrifices were the main parts of pagan worship.
    
5. **Семь богов из «Повести временных лет»**  
    — The 12th-century _Russian Primary Chronicle_ enumerates **seven Russian pagan divinities**: **Perun, Volos, Khors, Dazhbog, Stribog, Simargl, and Mokosh**[](https://www.britannica.com/topic/Slavic-religion).
    
6. **Сварог — бог-кузнец**  
    — One of the most ancient Slavic deities is **Svarog**, the divine blacksmith and father of the sun god Dazhbog. His festival was celebrated on September 8—9, when even the most forbidden magical acts were permitted.
    

#### ✨ МАГИЯ, СУЩЕСТВА И СОВРЕМЕННЫЕ ПЕРЕОСМЫСЛЕНИЯ (MAGIC, CREATURES & MODERN INTERPRETATIONS)

7. **Баба-Яга**  
    — **Baba Yaga**, one of the most well-known demons in Slavic beliefs, appears as an **ugly and deformed woman** who lives in a hut that stands on chicken legs. She has counterparts in German (_Frau Holle_), Italian (_Befana_), and Greek (_Circe_) mythology[](https://simple.wikipedia.org/wiki/Slavic_paganism).
    
8. **Волколак / Wilkolak (оборотень)**  
    — Slavic beliefs about _wilkolak_ (werewolf) included that a person could transform voluntarily or be cursed, and the condition could be passed through blood. The name is closely related to terms for "wolf's fur" and "fur coat"[](https://simple.wikipedia.org/wiki/Slavic_paganism).
    
9. **Радогост / Radegast**  
    — The West Slavic deity **Radegast** (god of hospitality and war) gave his name to a famous 7th—10th century cultic center, which contained many bronze and wooden idols. The god was associated with the sun and sacred horses.
    
10. **Сварожич — огонь**  
    — **Svarozhich** (son of Svarog) is the personification of fire, both hearth-fire and the lightning of the sky. In 12th-century chronicles, they mention "they bake pies to Svarozhich." The cult of Svarozhich persisted the longest near open flames in forges.
    
11. **Велес (Veles) — скотий бог**  
    — **Veles** was the god of the underworld, cattle, commerce, and poetry. The Christian Chronicle mentions that pagans "set separate tables for Veles" and swore oaths upon his name, sealing important treaties.
    
12. **Лихорадки-лихоманки (fever-demons)**  
    — _Likhomanki_ were nine fever-demons, daughters of the biblical King Herod. The apocryphal prayer "Sisinia's Legend" was the main weapon against them; apotropaic jewelry often had these texts.
    
13. **Здухачи / Zduhacz (wind-spirit summoners)**  
    — Among the South Slavs, _Zduhacz_ are living humans who can separate their souls from their bodies and, in the form of wind, fight enemy warriors or protect their villages from storms — essentially a belief in shaman-battlers of the winds.
    
14. **The Witcher franchise (books, games, TV series)**  
    — Andrzej Sapkowski's _The Witcher_ directly draws on Slavic mythological creatures and demonology: **strigas, kikimoras, leshys, noonwraiths**. The books and games are widely credited with popularizing Slavic folklore globally.
    
15. **«Слов'янські мавки» (Slavic Mavka)**  
    — Mavkas are forest spirits who lure men into the woods and tickle them to death. In 2023, the animated film **"Mavka. The Forest Song"** (based on Lesya Ukrainka's play) became a worldwide hit, introducing the image of Mavka as a gentle nature guardian.
    
16. **Баба-Яга в видеоиграх**  
    — In the **«Quest for Glory»** game series (1992), Baba Yaga appears as a major quest-giver and antagonist. In recent indie games (_Black Book_, _Tannenberg_), she is often reinterpreted as either a terrifying hag, a keeper of dark secrets, or a neutral figure of judgment.
    
17. **Вий (Viy) в кино**  
    — The demonic creature **"Viy"** from Gogol's story — the king of dwarfs with eyelids down to the ground — was first adapted for film in the 1967 Soviet cult classic _Viy_. The 2014 remake _Viy: Forbidden Empire_ became a commercial hit across Russia and China.
    
18. **The Epic of King Gesar**  
    — This epic, shared with much of Central Asia and Tibet, includes battles with **Mangas** (demons with stone heads) and motifs of wolf and falcon ancestors[](https://webarchiveweb.wayback.bac-lac.canada.ca/web/20210518153845/https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mongol_mythology).
    

#### 🌟 НИШЕВЫЙ ФАКТ О СЛАВЯНСКОЙ МАГИИ

19. **Роды и магия**  
    — The goddess **Mokosh** was the protector of women in childbirth. At the moment of birth, the umbilical cord was cut on the father's axe (if expecting a son) or on a distaff (for a daughter), simultaneously "programming" the child's gender and life path[](https://simple.wikipedia.org/wiki/Slavic_paganism).
